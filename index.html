Why use fun foam printing?

  • Easy to try different colours and patterns
  • Connects your handmade work with digital design
  • Looks professional in your portfolio

HOMEWORK: Make Your Foam Stamp Prints

Do this before class.

How to Make Your Print

1
Prepare the Foam
  • Cut the foam to the size you want
  • Clean it to remove any dust
2
Draw Your Design
  • Use a ballpoint pen
  • Press hard to make lines in the foam
  • The lines you draw will NOT have ink
  • The flat parts WILL have ink

💡 Tips

  • Press hard so the lines are deep
  • Simple designs work best
3
Add Ink
  • Press the ink pad onto the foam
  • Make sure all the flat parts have ink
  • The lines you drew should have no ink
4
Print
  • Put the foam face down on paper
  • Press down evenly on the back
  • Lift up to see your print
5
Experiment

Make at least 3 different prints. Try:

  • Different patterns
  • Different colours
  • Printing on top of each other (layering)

TASK 1: Edit Your Print in Photoshop

Clean your scanned print and prepare it for your research page.

📺 Watch This Video

Follow along with these steps:

Photoshop Steps

1
Open Your Scan
  • Open Photoshop
  • Go to File > Open
  • Find your scanned print
2
Make the Background White
  • Go to Image > Adjustments > Levels
  • Move the white slider to make the background white
  • This removes grey from the paper
3
Remove White Areas
  • Use the Rectangular Marquee Tool
  • Click on a white area
  • Go to Select > Similar
  • Press Delete on your keyboard
  • Press Ctrl + D (or Cmd + D on Mac) to deselect
Why do this? Now only your print is left. The white background is gone. You can put your print on any background.
4
Clean Up
  • Use the Eraser Tool to remove unwanted marks
  • Clean any dust or dirt from the scan
5
Try Effects (Optional)
  • Change colour: Go to Image > Adjustments > Hue/Saturation
  • Copy layer: Press Ctrl + J (or Cmd + J on Mac)
  • Add shadow: Go to Layer > Layer Style > Drop Shadow

TASK 2: Build a Research Page

Put your print into an A3 research page with annotation.

How to Make Your Research Page

1
Open Your A3 Scan Sheet
  • Open your A3 scan sheet in Photoshop
  • This will be your background
2
Add Your Print
  • Open your edited print from Task 1
  • Copy it: Ctrl + C (or Cmd + C on Mac)
  • Paste it on your A3 sheet: Ctrl + V (or Cmd + V on Mac)
  • Move and resize it: Ctrl + T (or Cmd + T on Mac)
3
Use Blending Modes
  • Click on your print layer
  • Find the blending mode dropdown (says "Normal")
  • Try Multiply — this makes white disappear
  • Try other modes to see what looks good
4
Add Annotation

Your research page needs:

  • Subheadings — titles for each section
  • Annotation — notes about your process and ideas
  • References — where you got inspiration from
5
Experiment
  • Try different layouts
  • Add more prints or images
  • Play with colours and effects
What to include on your research page:
  • Your foam prints — edited and clean
  • Subheadings — to organise your page
  • Annotation — explain what you did and why
  • References — artist names or image sources
